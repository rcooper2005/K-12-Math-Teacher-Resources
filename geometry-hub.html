üìö Lesson 1: Triangle Congruence Proofs

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will identify which congruence criterion applies
  • Students will write two-column proofs
  • Students will use CPCTC to prove parts congruent

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): Review the five criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL). "Given information tells us certain parts are equal. Can we conclude the triangles are congruent?"

Guided Practice (18 min): Work through proof together. Identify given, mark diagram, determine criterion, write proof. Use CPCTC (Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent) to prove additional parts.

Independent Practice (20 min): Students complete proofs with varying difficulty.

Closure (5 min): "What's the difference between SSS and SAS? When would you use each?"

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will define sine, cosine, and tangent ratios
  • Students will set up and solve trig equations
  • Students will find unknown side lengths in right triangles

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): All right triangles with a given acute angle are similar! This means the ratios of sides are always the same. Define: sin Œ∏ = opp/hyp, cos Œ∏ = adj/hyp, tan Œ∏ = opp/adj. Mnemonic: SOH-CAH-TOA.

Guided Practice (18 min): Label sides (opposite, adjacent, hypotenuse). Given angle and one side, find another: "If angle is 35¬∞ and hypotenuse is 10, find opposite." sin 35¬∞ = x/10, so x = 10¬∑sin 35¬∞.

Independent Practice (20 min): Solve for missing sides using all three ratios.

Closure (5 min): "How do you decide which ratio to use?"

  • Students will find missing angles using circle properties
  • Students will apply theorems to solve problems

Lesson Procedure

üìö Lesson 4: Similar Triangles and Indirect Measurement

üìö The Power of Proof

Euclid, a Greek mathematician around 300 BCE, wrote "The Elements" - one of the most influential books ever written. He showed that all of geometry could be built from just five simple assumptions (postulates)!

A mathematical proof is a logical argument that shows a statement must be true. Unlike science, which uses evidence, mathematics uses pure logic. Once proven, a theorem is true forever - Euclid's proofs are still valid 2,300 years later!

üìö Trigonometry: Measuring the Unmeasurable

How did ancient Egyptians build the pyramids so precisely? How do we know the distance to stars? Trigonometry - the study of triangle relationships!

The word "trigonometry" comes from Greek: "trigonon" (triangle) + "metron" (measure). By knowing just one side and one angle of a right triangle, we can find everything else. This power lets us measure distances we could never reach directly.

‚ùì Common Misconceptions

‚ùå SSA proves congruence

Error: Using two sides and non-included angle as proof

Correction: SSA is the "ambiguous case" - can give 0, 1, or 2 triangles!

‚ùå Confusing adjacent and opposite sides

Error: Labeling sides incorrectly for trig ratios

Correction: Always label from the angle's perspective. Opposite is across from angle; adjacent is next to angle (not hypotenuse).

‚ùå Scale factor applies same to area

Error: If lengths double, area doubles

Correction: If lengths multiply by k, area multiplies by k¬≤. Double sides = quadruple area!
