The Foundation for Algebra

Understanding y = mx + b

m = slope: Rate of change; rise/run; steepness and direction

b = y-intercept: Where line crosses y-axis; starting value

Key Connections
  • Slope = constant rate of change = constant of proportionality (when b=0)
  • Proportional: y = mx (through origin)
  • Non-proportional: y = mx + b, b ‚â† 0

üîÑ Transformations

Rigid Motions and Dilations

Rigid Motions (Congruence)
  • Translation: Slide (same size, same orientation)
  • Reflection: Flip over a line (mirror image)
  • Rotation: Turn around a point

Rigid motions preserve size and shape ‚Üí congruent figures

Dilation (Similarity)

Enlargement or reduction by scale factor k

k > 1: enlargement; 0 < k < 1: reduction

Dilations preserve shape but not size ‚Üí similar figures

üî¢ Systems of Equations

Solving Two Equations Simultaneously

Methods
  • Graphing: Solution is intersection point
  • Substitution: Replace variable with equivalent expression
  • Elimination: Add/subtract equations to eliminate variable
Types of Solutions
  • One solution: Lines intersect (different slopes)
  • No solution: Parallel lines (same slope, different intercept)
  • Infinite solutions: Same line (equivalent equations)

üìö Lesson 1: Understanding Slope

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will calculate slope from two points using rise/run
  • Students will interpret slope as rate of change
  • Students will identify positive, negative, zero, and undefined slopes

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): Show staircase image. "How steep are these stairs?" Introduce slope as rise/run. m = (y‚ÇÇ-y‚ÇÅ)/(x‚ÇÇ-x‚ÇÅ).

Guided Practice (18 min): Calculate slope from graphs and from two points. Connect to real-world rates: "The pool fills at 30 gallons per minute" ‚Üí slope = 30.

Independent Practice (20 min): Find slopes from various representations. Identify slope types.

Closure (5 min): "What does a negative slope mean in context?"

üìö Lesson 2: The Pythagorean Theorem

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will state and apply the Pythagorean Theorem
  • Students will solve for unknown sides in right triangles
  • Students will verify the theorem using area models

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): Draw right triangle with squares on each side. "The areas of the two smaller squares add up to the area of the largest square!" Demonstrate with 3-4-5 triangle: 9 + 16 = 25.

Guided Practice (18 min): Practice finding missing sides. Given a=6, b=8, find c. Given a=5, c=13, find b. Emphasize: c is ALWAYS the hypotenuse (longest side, across from right angle).

Independent Practice (20 min): Mixed practice finding unknown sides.

Closure (5 min): "A ladder reaches 12 feet up a wall. It's placed 5 feet from the wall. How long is the ladder?"

üìö Lesson 3: Transformations on the Coordinate Plane

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will perform translations, reflections, and rotations
  • Students will describe transformations using coordinate notation
  • Students will identify transformation from coordinates

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): Review three types. Translation: (x, y) ‚Üí (x+a, y+b). Reflection over y-axis: (x, y) ‚Üí (-x, y). Rotation 90¬∞ clockwise: (x, y) ‚Üí (y, -x).

Guided Practice (18 min): Transform triangles on coordinate plane. Verify with tracing paper. Write transformation rules.

Independent Practice (20 min): Perform transformations; identify transformations from before/after coordinates.

Closure (5 min): "Point A(3, -2) is reflected over the x-axis. What are the new coordinates?"

üìö Lesson 4: Solving Systems by Graphing

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will solve systems of equations by graphing
  • Students will interpret the intersection point
  • Students will recognize parallel and coincident lines

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): "Two cell phone plans: A charges $20 + $0.05/text. B charges $30 + $0.02/text. When are they the same cost?" Write equations: y = 0.05x + 20, y = 0.02x + 30.

Guided Practice (18 min): Graph both lines. Find intersection: approximately (333, 36.67). At about 333 texts, both cost about $37. Verify by substitution.

Independent Practice (20 min): Solve systems by graphing. Include examples with no solution and infinite solutions.

Closure (5 min): "What does it mean if two lines are parallel when solving a system?"

üìö The Pythagorean Theorem: Ancient Wisdom

Over 2,500 years ago, the Greek mathematician Pythagoras discovered something amazing about right triangles. If you draw a square on each side of a right triangle, the areas of the two smaller squares always add up to the area of the largest square!

Written as an equation: a¬≤ + b¬≤ = c¬≤, where c is the longest side (hypotenuse). This simple formula lets us find distances we can't measure directly - like the diagonal of a room or the straight-line distance between two points on a map.

üìö What Makes a Function?

A function is like a machine: put something in, get exactly one thing out. If you put in 3 and sometimes get 6 but sometimes get 9, that's NOT a function!

The "Vertical Line Test" checks if a graph is a function: if any vertical line crosses the graph more than once, it's not a function. Why? That would mean one input gives multiple outputs.

Functions are everywhere: your age is a function of the year you were born. The cost of gas is a function of how many gallons you buy.

üìñ Math Vocabulary

Definition: Rate of change; rise over run; m in y = mx + b

"A line through (0,1) and (2,5) has slope = 4/2 = 2."

Y-intercept

Definition: Where a line crosses the y-axis; b in y = mx + b

"In y = 3x - 2, the y-intercept is -2."

Hypotenuse

Definition: Longest side of a right triangle (across from right angle)

"In a¬≤ + b¬≤ = c¬≤, c is the hypotenuse."

Irrational Number

Definition: A number that cannot be written as a fraction

"‚àö2, œÄ, and ‚àö3 are irrational - decimals never end or repeat."

Function

Definition: A rule that assigns exactly one output to each input

"y = 2x + 1 is a function; each x gives one y."

System of Equations

Definition: Two or more equations considered together

"y = 2x + 1 and y = -x + 7 form a system; solution is (2, 5)."

Definition: A transformation that slides a figure without rotating or flipping

"(x, y) ‚Üí (x+3, y-2) translates right 3, down 2."

‚ùì Common Misconceptions

Correction: Slope = rise/run = 4/2 = 2. It's a ratio!

‚ùå Using Pythagorean Theorem on non-right triangles

Correction: ONLY works for RIGHT triangles! Check for 90¬∞ angle first.

‚ùå All square roots are irrational
