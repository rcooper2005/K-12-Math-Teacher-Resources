Deepening Understanding

What Makes a Relationship Proportional?
  • Two quantities have a constant ratio
  • Graph passes through the origin (0, 0)
  • Can be expressed as y = kx where k is constant of proportionality
Representations
  • Table: Ratios are equivalent across rows
  • Graph: Straight line through origin
  • Equation: y = kx
  • Verbal: "y is k times x"
Percent Applications

All percent problems are proportional relationships!

  • Tax: total = price + (price √ó rate)
  • Discount: sale = original - (original √ó rate)

Extending to All Rationals

Adding/Subtracting Integers

Same sign: Add absolute values, keep sign. (-5) + (-3) = -8

Different signs: Subtract absolute values, keep sign of larger. (-7) + 4 = -3

Subtracting: Add the opposite. 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8

Multiplying/Dividing Integers

Same sign: Positive result. (-4)(-3) = 12

Different signs: Negative result. (-4)(3) = -12

Division follows same rules.

üìö Lesson 1: Constant of Proportionality

‚è±Ô∏è 55 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will identify the constant of proportionality in tables, graphs, and equations
  • Students will write equations for proportional relationships
  • Students will explain what k represents in context

Lesson Procedure

Introduction (12 min): Present table: Hours worked (2, 4, 6) ‚Üí Money earned ($24, $48, $72). "What's the relationship?" Every hour = $12. This constant rate is k.

Independent Practice (20 min): Identify k in various representations. Write equations. Interpret k in context.

Closure (5 min): "How do you know a relationship is proportional?"

üìö Lesson 4: Solving Two-Step Equations

‚è±Ô∏è 50 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Students will solve equations requiring two operations
  • Students will write equations from word problems
  • Students will check solutions

Lesson Procedure

üìö The Magic Number: œÄ

Every circle, no matter how big or small, shares a magical property. If you divide the distance around any circle (circumference) by the distance across it (diameter), you always get the same number: œÄ (pi), approximately 3.14159...

œÄ never ends and never repeats! Mathematicians have calculated trillions of digits, but it goes on forever. This makes œÄ irrational - it can't be written as a simple fraction.

üìö Why Negative √ó Negative = Positive

This rule seems strange, but patterns prove it! Start with 3 √ó 2 = 6. Now decrease the second number: 3 √ó 1 = 3, 3 √ó 0 = 0, 3 √ó (-1) = -3. Each time, we subtract 3.

Now do the same starting with -3 √ó 2 = -6. Then -3 √ó 1 = -3, -3 √ó 0 = 0. Following the pattern, -3 √ó (-1) = 3! Each time we add 3.

The pattern forces negative √ó negative to be positive!

‚úÖ Appropriate Uses (with Teacher Permission)

  • Check your completed work: Compare your solution process with AI's approach
  • Get alternative explanations: "Explain Proportions, Rational Numbers, Probability a different way"
  • Understand word problems: Help interpreting what a problem is asking (not solving it)

  • Generate practice problems: Ask for more problems like your homework to practice
  • Clarify vocabulary: "What does 'coefficient' mean in this context?"
üö´ NOT Appropriate Uses
  • Getting answers without trying: This is cheating AND means you don't learn
  • Copying solutions for homework: Your teacher will know, and you won't learn
  • Using during tests or quizzes: This is academic dishonesty
  • Skipping steps you're supposed to show: The work IS the learning

üîç Critical Evaluation Skills for Math AI

How to Check if AI is Right
  • Substitute back: Put your answer back into the original equation
  • Estimate first: Does the AI's answer make sense based on your estimate?
  • Check each step: AI often makes errors in the middle of solutions
  • Graph it: Use Desmos to visually verify algebraic solutions
  • Try a different tool: Compare answers from Wolfram Alpha, Photomath, etc.
Common AI Math Errors to Watch For
  • Sign errors (positive/negative mistakes)
  • Distributing incorrectly
  • Misreading the original problem
  • Skipping steps that are actually required
  • Getting word problems completely wrong

üö´ What AI Cannot Replace

  • The learning IS in the struggle: When you're confused and work through it, your brain grows
  • Number sense and intuition: Only develops through practice with numbers
  • Problem-solving strategies: You learn these by solving problems, not watching AI solve them
  • Showing YOUR thinking: Your teacher needs to see how YOU reason
  • Preparing for tests: On tests, it's just you and your brain - build those skills now
