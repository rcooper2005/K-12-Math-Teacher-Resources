‚ùì Common Misconceptions

‚ùå "95% confident the true mean is in this interval"

Correct: "We are 95% confident that the interval captures the true mean." The parameter is fixed; the interval varies from sample to sample.

‚ùå "p-value is the probability H‚ÇÄ is true"

Correct: p-value is the probability of observing data this extreme IF H‚ÇÄ were true. H‚ÇÄ is either true or false - it doesn't have a probability.

‚ùå Correlation implies causation

Correct: Only randomized experiments can establish causation. Observational studies can only show association.
