Beyond Quadratics

Key Concepts
  • Degree: Highest power determines end behavior and max zeros
  • Leading coefficient: Determines end behavior direction
  • Multiplicity: How many times factor appears; affects graph behavior at zero
End Behavior
  • Even degree + positive leading: both ends up (‚Üó‚Üñ)
  • Even degree + negative leading: both ends down (‚Üò‚Üô)
  • Odd degree + positive leading: down-left, up-right (‚Üô‚Üó)
  • Odd degree + negative leading: up-left, down-right (‚Üñ‚Üò)
Important Theorems
  • Fundamental Theorem: Degree n polynomial has exactly n zeros (counting multiplicity, including complex)
  • Factor Theorem: (x - c) is factor iff f(c) = 0
  • Rational Root Theorem: Possible rational roots = ¬±(factors of constant)/(factors of leading coefficient)

üìä Logarithmic Functions

The Inverse of Exponentials

Definition

log_b(x) = y means b^y = x

"log_b(x) is the exponent you put on b to get x"

Properties of Logarithms
  • log_b(MN) = log_b(M) + log_b(N)
  • log_b(M/N) = log_b(M) - log_b(N)
  • log_b(M^p) = p ¬∑ log_b(M)
  • log_b(b) = 1 and log_b(1) = 0
Change of Base

log_b(x) = ln(x)/ln(b) = log(x)/log(b)

üìö Logarithms: Taming Large Numbers

John Napier invented logarithms in 1614 to simplify astronomical calculations. Before calculators, multiplying large numbers took hours. With logs, multiplication becomes addition!

Today, logarithms help us understand phenomena that span huge ranges: earthquake intensity varies by factors of millions, as does sound intensity. Logarithmic scales compress these ranges into manageable numbers.

üìö Trigonometry: Mathematics of Waves

Sine and cosine aren't just about triangles - they describe any repeating phenomenon! Sound waves, light waves, ocean tides, and even your heartbeat can all be modeled with trigonometric functions.

Fourier discovered that ANY periodic wave can be built from sine and cosine waves. This discovery revolutionized physics, engineering, and music - it's why we can compress MP3 files and analyze MRI scans!
